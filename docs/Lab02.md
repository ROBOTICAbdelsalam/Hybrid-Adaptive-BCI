# Background

Electroencephalography (EEG) records electrical activity generated by the brain using electrodes placed on the scalp. EEG signals are non-invasive, have high temporal resolution, and are commonly used in Brain–Computer Interface (BCI) systems.

The EEGBCI dataset, provided by PhysioNet and supported by the MNE-Python library, contains standardized EEG recordings collected during motor tasks. It is one of the most commonly used public datasets for developing and evaluating BCI algorithms.

Using a public benchmark dataset ensures reproducibility and allows comparison with previously published research.
